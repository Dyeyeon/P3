Pavilion

Spatial Design

The pavilion is a music recital with a piano located on the first floor. Our goal is to design so that people can go up to the second floor as well as the first floor to listen to music through the glass floor and watch the piano.

SCHOOL PROJECT (Team Project)

Ideation, rhino, grasshopper, and blogging were my roles. The musical recital pavilion is designed to recreate structures inspired by the bass clef shape that create aesthetically pleasing, functional, and satisfying human experiences.

SKILLS/TOOLS

Goal for Pavilion

A pavilion is a musical recital that includes a piano and is designed to let people listen to music and see the piano. It is a structure that is aesthetically satisfying by utilizing the shape of the bass clef and designed in consideration of vertical and horizontal circulation, creating a satisfactory human experience so that people can move freely between seats and the first and second floors.

Ideation

These are the isometric sketches of the pavilion. But we didn't think this shape was suitable for showing the musical building, so we made two rhino models besides these sketches.

The first alternative model is the bass clef shape model. It consists of two floors and shows the first floor, the second floor, and the ceiling in a total of three identical bass clef shapes. On the first floor, people can listen to music through the half-open floor, and they can go up to the second floor using the spiral stairs and listen to music while watching play the piano.

Our second alternative model is the shape of the piano. The basic shape of the model expresses the shape of the grand piano from above and the ceiling as the opened piano. It is also composed of two floors and all horizontal planes are piano shaped. People can go up to the second floor using the stairs and listen to music while watching play the piano.

After First Feedback

We decided to make our final model a bass clef-shaped model through feedback that the bass clef-shaped model is attractive.

In addition, due to the lack of seats on the second floor of the previous model, the position of the piano was moved to the first floor, the floor of the first floor was changed into a circle, and chairs were placed on both the first and second floors so that people can listen to the music and watch the piano.

Moreover, half of the wall was glass with frames so that people passing by outside can see the piano indoors

After Second Feedback

We used Twinmotion last week to add a real human model to our pavilion and realized that people could not sit on the second floor and watch the piano. Through Ken's feedback, we were able to solve our problem by changing the material of the second floor to glass. Our model also utilized layered architecture and infill architecture in Syntactic Feature, where we added a thinner frame to the glass frame and added colorful glass to show a more enhanced infill architecture. In addition, a thicker horizontal frame and a thinner vertical frame of the glass frame were added to better represent the layered architecture

Reflection

Digital models can be easily seen as finished products compared to physical models and were able to adjust and change freely, quickly, and accurately, such as the ratio, width, and height we wanted. In addition, there is an advantage that can be easily seen by changing the material differently for each desired part in our pavilion model. On the other hand, the physical model allows us to manipulate the shape or complex shape we want more carefully. Using the physical model would have been preferable in terms of using the desired functionality (boolean, join, exit, trim, etc.) The digital model did not function as we had hope.
